Attendance: check-in & check-out

Article 5 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

Attendance is the most-used feature of the Portal. Every parent uses it every school day. This article covers how the check-in and check-out flows work, what data is captured, and how it shows up on your admin dashboard.

The Attendance page

When a parent taps Attendance in the nav, they see a card for each enrolled student in the family. Each card shows:

  • The student’s name and classroom
  • Today’s status: Not yet Present Picked up
  • The current sign-in / pick-up time, if set
  • A button: Check in or Check out, depending on state

Check-in flow

The parent taps Check in for a student. They’re taken to a clean form with:

  • The student’s name confirmed at the top
  • An optional Notes for school staff textarea (slept poorly, new med, late breakfast, etc.)
  • A signature pad where the parent signs with finger or mouse
  • A Confirm drop-off button

On submit, the student’s status flips to Present, the timestamp is recorded, and the parent is returned to the Attendance page. The whole flow takes about 15 seconds.

Check-out flow

At pick-up, the parent taps Check out. The form is slightly bigger because school policy usually requires recording who picked up:

  • The student’s name confirmed at the top
  • A Who is picking up? selector with three kinds of options:
    • Myself — the parent signing in
    • Other parent — any other parent on the family record
    • Authorized pickup person — anyone the family has added to their authorized list (see Article 6)
  • A Curbside pickup checkbox
  • An optional Notes for school staff textarea
  • A signature pad
  • A Confirm pick-up button

On submit, the student’s status flips to Picked up, the time and the named pickup person are recorded.

Attendance events are append-only: once recorded, they can’t be edited or deleted. Corrections happen by adding a new event (e.g. an admin manual override), which preserves the original record. This protects you for audit and dispute purposes.

Where staff see the data

On the admin side, your Attendance dashboard shows:

  • A live feed of recent events for today, with timestamps and signatures — including any notes parents wrote
  • A per-classroom roster of who’s present, absent, or yet-to-arrive
  • Per-student history with full event log
  • Notes appear as a 📝 badge on the student’s row so the front desk doesn’t miss them

What about notes parents leave?

The optional Notes for school staff textarea is small but powerful. Common uses:

  • “Didn’t sleep well, please go easy on him today.”
  • “New allergy — please update file.”
  • “Grandpa is picking up tomorrow.”
  • “Gave Tylenol at 7am.”

Notes are visible to your staff in three places on the admin Attendance dashboard: inline in the recent-events feed, in the per-student accordion drawer, and as a hover-tooltip on the row badge.

What about absences?

Today, parents don’t mark absences in the portal — if a parent doesn’t check their student in, they show up on the staff roster as Not yet. School staff can mark a student absent from the admin dashboard if needed. A parent-side absence flow is on the roadmap.

What happens if a parent forgets to check out?

The student stays in Present state for that day. School staff can mark the check-out from the admin dashboard the next day (which writes a corrective manual-override event). Most schools train their teachers to do a quick end-of-day roster check.

What about pick-ups by authorized non-parents?

If a parent isn’t the one picking up — say a grandparent or a nanny — the parent still signs in advance from their portal selecting the authorized person, then the school staff verifies ID on arrival. The portal record establishes that the family authorized the pickup, and the staff record establishes the in-person verification.

See Article 6 — Authorized pickup people for how parents manage their list.
