Messages

Article 9 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

The Messages tab is the direct line between a family and your school office. It’s the “short, useful, two-way” channel that sits between formal forms and a phone call. This article covers how it works for parents, what your office sees, and how to use it well.

How it works for parents

When a parent opens the Messages tab, they see:

  • The full thread of messages between their family and the school, newest at the top.
  • A clear visual distinction between school messages and parent messages.
  • Timestamps on every message.
  • A compose box at the top to send a new message.

The conversation is per-family, not per-parent. If mom and dad both have portal accounts on the same family, they see the same thread. Either one can reply.

How it works for school staff

Messages from parents flow into your school’s admin Conversations area inside Growth Suite. Staff reply from there exactly the way they reply to any other contact conversation — same compose box, same templates, same attachments. The reply appears in the parent’s portal Messages tab almost instantly.

This is intentional — staff don’t need a separate “Parent Portal Messages” inbox. The portal is just another channel feeding the same conversation history the staff already uses.

Notifications

When the school replies to a parent message, the parent receives an email notification (a short “You have a new message from [school name]” with a link to the portal). They don’t need to log in proactively to see new messages.

On the parent side, the Messages tab shows an unread count on the navigation when there are messages they haven’t seen yet, so even without email they’ll see the badge next time they open the portal.

What to use Messages for

Messages is best for short, two-way communication where a written record is useful:

  • “Sara has a doctor’s appointment Thursday at 2pm — we’ll pick her up early.”
  • “Can you confirm whether Jake’s allergy form is on file for this year?”
  • “Sending grandma to pick up today — she’ll arrive around 3:15.”
  • “Hi! New phone number is 555-0123. Could you update it?”
  • School replies, follow-ups, questions about a form they filled out.

What not to use Messages for

To avoid clutter or confusion, redirect parents away from Messages for:

Don’t use Messages forUse this instead
Emergencies (child sick at school, lockdown, weather)Phone. Always.
Permission slips and acknowledgementsPortal Forms
Sending the school a documentDocuments upload
Daily attendance check-in or pickup notesAttendance check-in/out with the note field
Bulk school-wide announcementsYour usual newsletter / email blast tool

Group conversations and the family thread

Because the conversation is per-family, both parents on a household see the same thread. If your school replies to a message from mom, dad sees the reply too. This prevents the common “one parent told the school X, the other parent doesn’t know” breakdown.

Tone and templates

Most schools establish 3–5 message templates for the front desk to use as starting points (acknowledging a form was received, confirming a pickup, requesting a missing document). Growth Suite’s admin Conversations area supports saved replies — talk to your account contact about getting templates set up.

Retention & privacy

  • The full message history is retained as long as the family is enrolled (and beyond, for record-keeping).
  • Messages are visible to all staff with admin access to your school. They’re not shared with other schools or third parties.
  • Parents can scroll back through years of conversation if they need to find something.

Reminder: Messages are not a substitute for a phone call in an actual emergency. Encourage parents to call the school directly for anything urgent — the front desk doesn’t sit in the Messages inbox continuously.
