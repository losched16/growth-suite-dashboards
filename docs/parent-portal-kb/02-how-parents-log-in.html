How parents log in

Article 2 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

The Parent Portal uses passwordless magic-link login. Parents never create or remember a password. Instead, they enter their email, we send them a one-tap link, and they’re in. This article explains the flow, why we built it that way, and how to support parents who get stuck.

The login flow, step by step

1

The parent opens the portal in their browser and is shown a single field: Email.

2

They type the email address you have on file for them and tap Send me a sign-in link.

3

Within a few seconds, they receive an email with the subject line “Sign in to [your school name]”. The body has a single big button: Sign in to your portal.

4

They tap the button. The portal opens, they’re signed in. The session lasts 30 days — on that device they won’t need to sign in again for a month.

Why no passwords?

Parents log in to the school portal a few times a month at most. Every time you add a password to anything, the most likely outcome is “parent forgets it, parent emails the office, office resets it.” Magic links remove the password entirely — the parent’s email account itself becomes the security boundary. This is the same approach used by Slack’s family-of-apps invitations, Substack, and many modern banking apps.

The practical wins:

  • No password reset tickets for your office.
  • No insecure password choices (“School123”, “Mason2024”).
  • No password manager fights for parents on shared family devices.
  • Faster login on mobile — no autofill problems.

What email gets the link?

The link goes to whatever email address is on file for that parent in Growth Suite. For a parent to log in, two things must be true:

  1. They are listed as an active parent on a family record in Growth Suite.
  2. That parent record has a valid email address.

If either is missing, the magic-link request will silently fail (no email is sent). This is by design — we don’t want to leak whether an email is “known” to your school.

What you should tell families on day one

Send this in your launch email:

“Your portal is at [your portal URL]. To sign in, enter the email address we have on file for you. We’ll email you a one-tap sign-in link — no password to remember. Bookmark the page once you’re signed in. The portal works on phones, tablets, and computers.”

Common login questions parents ask

QuestionAnswer
“I never got the email.” Check spam/promotions. If it’s not there after 5 minutes, the email on file probably doesn’t match what they typed. The school can verify and update.
“Do my spouse and I share a login?” No — each parent has their own login tied to their own email. They both see the same family data. Both can take any action (sign a form, check a kid in, message the school).
“The link expired.” Magic links expire after a short window. Just request a new one — takes 5 seconds.
“Can I log in from a different device?” Yes. The portal works on any modern browser. Each device is its own 30-day session.
“Do I need to download an app?” No. It’s a website that works perfectly on a phone’s browser. They can “Add to Home Screen” for an app-like icon if they want.

What the portal address is

Each school has a stable parent portal URL set during onboarding. Once you have the URL, share it on your school’s public website, in your enrollment welcome email, in your parent handbook — anywhere parents will look for it.

Don’t share specific portal URLs that include parameters or tokens. Always share the bare login page (e.g. your-school.growthsuite.app), not a full link from your own session. If you copy a URL while logged in, it might contain something specific to your session that won’t work for someone else.
