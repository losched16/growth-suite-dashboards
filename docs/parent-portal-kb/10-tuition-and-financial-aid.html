Tuition & Financial Aid

Article 10 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

Two related areas inside the portal handle tuition and financial aid. They’re separate because the audiences are different — Tuition is for every paying family; Financial Aid is for families applying for or receiving tuition assistance. This article covers both.

Tuition

What parents see

The Tuition tab shows a snapshot of the family’s tuition status:

  • Current balance — what they owe right now.
  • Next payment due — date and amount.
  • Payment plan — if applicable, the schedule (monthly, quarterly, annually) and the total annual amount.
  • Payment history — past payments with dates, amounts, and what they were for.
  • Pay now — a link to your school’s payment processor, if you’ve enabled online payment.

Where the data comes from

The Tuition tab reflects what’s in your tuition-management system. If you use the built-in Growth Suite tuition tracking, the portal mirrors it. If you use an external system (FACTS, TADS, Smart Tuition, your own bookkeeping), the portal can either:

  • Show a summary that’s synced from the external system (preferred), or
  • Display a link out to the external system if no integration is set up

Reach out to your account contact about integrating your specific tuition system.

Online payment

If your school accepts online payments, the Pay now button links to your configured processor (Stripe, FACTS, or whatever you use). The portal doesn’t process payments directly — it’s a hand-off to your payment provider, which keeps PCI compliance simple.

Financial Aid (TuitionBridge)

What it is

TuitionBridge is Growth Suite’s financial aid module. It lets families apply for tuition assistance directly through the portal, and lets your school review, approve, and track those applications without a separate FA platform.

What parents see — first visit

A family that hasn’t applied yet sees a welcome panel explaining the financial aid process at your school, with a clear Start application button. Most schools write a brief paragraph about who qualifies and what’s required — that text is configurable per school.

What parents see — with an application in progress

  • Application status — in progress, submitted, under review, awarded, declined.
  • Required documents — a checklist of what they still need to upload (tax returns, W-2s, supporting docs).
  • Upload area — uploads tied to the FA application specifically.
  • Notes from the FA committee — if your school sends notes back during review.
  • Award decision — once a decision is made, the awarded amount and any conditions.
  • Accept / decline buttons on the award.

What school staff see

On the admin side, the Financial Aid Queue dashboard shows every application with:

  • Family name and contact info
  • Application status
  • Documents received vs missing
  • Household financial summary (computed from submitted docs)
  • Internal notes from your FA committee
  • Buttons to request more info, award an amount, or decline

Per-household vs per-student

Financial Aid applications are filed per household. One application covers all students enrolled in that family for the academic year. Award amounts can be allocated per student inside the household when needed.

Privacy

Financial aid is the most sensitive data in the Portal. A few defaults:

  • Only authenticated parents in the applying family can see their FA application.
  • School staff access is controlled by admin role — not every staff member has FA-queue access.
  • Uploaded financial documents are encrypted at rest and only accessible to authenticated FA reviewers.
  • FA data does not appear on general family-hub views — it’s siloed in its own dashboard.

Reminder: If your school doesn’t use TuitionBridge today, the Financial Aid tab in the parent portal is simply not shown to families. There’s no half-built empty state for families to stumble into.
