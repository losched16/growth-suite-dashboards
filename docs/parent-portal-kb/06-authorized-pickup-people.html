Authorized pickup people

Article 6 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

Every family has a list of authorized pickup people — non-parent adults who can come to school and pick up the children. Grandparents, aunts, nannies, trusted family friends. Parents manage this list themselves from inside their portal. This article explains how it works and what the school sees on the other end.

Where parents find it

In the parent portal, the list lives under Settings → Authorized pickup people. Parents see:

  • Every person currently on the list (name, relationship, phone)
  • A button to Add a new person
  • An edit / deactivate button on each existing person

What’s captured per person

FieldRequired?Notes
Full nameYesHow it appears on their ID.
Relationship to the student(s)Yese.g. Grandparent, Aunt, Nanny, Family friend.
Phone numberOptionalFor staff to call if there’s a question at pickup.
NotesOptionalAnything the school should know (e.g. “only on Wednesdays”, “parent will call ahead”).
Active flagDefault onSet to off to remove someone from the pickup options without deleting historical records.

How the list is shared in the family

The pickup list is shared across both parents in a household. If mom adds “Grandma Sue” to the list, dad sees Grandma Sue in his pickup-person selector too. This is intentional — it means a family only maintains one list, and either parent can authorize a pickup from any device.

How it appears at check-out

When a parent does a check-out from the Attendance page, the “Who is picking up?” selector shows three groups:

  1. The signing parent themselves (default selected)
  2. Other parents on the family record
  3. Active authorized pickup people

The parent picks the right option, signs the check-out, and submits. The signed event includes a snapshot of the pickup person’s name — so if the family later removes that person from the list, the historical pickup record still shows who actually picked the student up.

What school staff see

On the admin Attendance dashboard, each check-out event lists the pickup person by name. There’s no separate “pickup list” admin view today — the list is intentionally parent-managed so your office doesn’t become the bottleneck for every new grandparent.

Verification at pickup

The Portal’s pickup list is a digital authorization — it tells you the family approved this person. School staff still verify ID in person at pickup. Two things make this work smoothly:

  • The parent signs the check-out from their phone before sending the pickup person, so the event is on file before the person arrives.
  • The phone number on the pickup-person record gives staff a way to call back the family if anything seems off.

Custody and complicated families

Some families have legal restrictions about who can or can’t pick up. The portal gives you the authorization layer, but it doesn’t enforce custody orders. We recommend:

  • Ask families with custody arrangements to upload court orders via the Documents section (see Article 8).
  • Note any custody restrictions on the family record on the admin side, so staff see them.
  • Have your front-desk team confirm restricted pickup people verbally with the custodial parent on the first pickup.

Don’t use the Authorized Pickup List as legal documentation of custody. It’s an operational tool to make daily pickup smooth. Court orders, divorce decrees, and custody agreements should be on file as uploaded documents (see Article 8) and referenced in your school’s legal records.

What about the Authorization for Release form?

Many schools also have a dedicated Authorization for Release / Pickup Authorization Form as part of their annual paperwork. That’s a Portal Form that captures the list once a year with a signature for legal record-keeping. The two systems coexist:

  • The annual form is the legal record of authorization. Stored as a signed form.
  • The Settings list is the day-to-day operational list parents use during pickup. Updated as the family’s life changes.

Schools that want them in sync can ask parents to update both whenever someone is added or removed. Most schools just use the operational list and ask parents to re-confirm the legal form once a year.
