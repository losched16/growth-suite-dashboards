Helping a parent who’s stuck

Article 11 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

This is the troubleshooting article your front desk should bookmark. It covers the most common parent issues and how to resolve them in under a minute. If something here doesn’t fix the problem, escalate to your account contact.

1. “I can’t log in.”

First check: which email are they using?

Ask the parent which email they typed into the login screen. Open their family record in your admin dashboard and confirm that exact email is on file for them.

  • Match. The magic-link should have sent. Ask them to check spam and the “Promotions” tab in Gmail.
  • Mismatch. They’re typing an old email. Either update the email on file (if it’s changed for real) or have them try the correct email.
  • No email on file. Their parent record exists but has no email. Add one in your admin dashboard, then have them try.

Second check: did the email arrive?

  • Did it land in spam? Have them mark it “not spam” once so future ones land in inbox.
  • Did it get caught by a school-district firewall? (Common with .edu email addresses.) Suggest a personal email instead.
  • Did they click the link more than 15 minutes after it arrived? Magic links expire. Request a fresh one.

2. “I don’t see my student.”

The student must be tied to the family record the parent is signed in to. Check:

  • Is the student in your admin dashboard, tagged to this family?
  • Is the student’s status set to active? Withdrawn or pending students don’t show in the parent view.
  • Is the parent on the right family? Occasionally a parent has been moved between family records and we’re looking at the wrong one.

3. “The form won’t let me submit.”

Most form-submission failures are validation errors. Ask the parent what error message they saw — the portal always shows a specific reason.

  • “X is required.” A required field was empty. They need to scroll up and fill it in.
  • “Please sign before submitting.” They didn’t lock the drawn signature. Have them sign and tap Lock signature (the box turns green).
  • “File too large.” Their attached PDF or image is over the 10 MB limit. Suggest compressing or splitting into pages.
  • “Already submitted.” They’ve already submitted this one-per-year form. Check the History page — their submission is on file.

4. “I signed my child in but it shows them as not yet checked in.”

Three things to check, in order:

  1. Is the parent on the right child? In a family with multiple kids, sometimes a parent confirms the form thinking they hit child A when they hit child B.
  2. Refresh the page. The Attendance page is server-rendered, so a stale cached view from before the submission can sometimes show the old state.
  3. Look at the admin Attendance dashboard for the event. If you see the event there, the portal is just showing a stale view. If you don’t, the submission didn’t go through — have them try again.

5. “The site keeps signing me out.”

  • The session is 30 days. If they’re signed out earlier, they probably cleared their browser cookies or used a private/incognito window.
  • Some browsers (especially Safari on iOS) aggressively clear third-party cookies. Tell them to add the portal as a Home Screen icon — that pins the session.
  • Multiple devices is fine, but each device counts as its own 30-day session. If they’re bouncing between phone, work laptop, and home iPad, they’ll log in fresh on each one.

6. “My uploaded document is stuck on ‘sending…’”

Documents push to your admin Conversations area in the background. If the push fails (rare), the status badge shows syncing… and the system retries automatically. In all cases the file is safely stored on the Portal side — you can download it from the admin Family Uploads dashboard regardless of sync status.

7. “I don’t see the Financial Aid tab.”

The Financial Aid tab only appears if your school uses TuitionBridge. If a family should see it but doesn’t, check that TuitionBridge is enabled for your school and that the family has been marked as eligible.

8. “A field in my profile is wrong but I can’t edit it.”

Some fields are intentionally not parent-editable (legal name, date of birth, classroom, enrollment status). For those, ask the parent to send a Messages note or call the office — your staff can update the record on the admin side.

9. “The page won’t load.”

  • Have them refresh once.
  • Have them try a different browser. (Chrome and Safari are the most-tested.)
  • Have them try a different device (phone vs computer).
  • If still broken on multiple devices, escalate — it’s probably a real outage and your account contact needs to know.

10. “I’m getting locked out / the form keeps clearing.”

Most often this is a session-expired situation. The portal is generally tolerant — if a parent’s session expires mid-form, the form data is lost. Tell them:

  • Log in fresh.
  • Fill out the form in one session without leaving the tab idle for 30+ minutes.
  • Use the “Lock signature” step early once they’ve signed, so they don’t lose work.

When to escalate

If you’ve tried the steps above and the parent is still stuck, escalate to your account contact with:

  • The parent’s name and email.
  • What they were trying to do.
  • What error message (if any) they saw.
  • What device + browser they were using.
  • A timestamp (“happened around 8:15am today”) so we can look at server logs.

Tip: The single most common cause of “I can’t log in” is a typo in the email the parent is entering. Save 15 minutes per ticket by having them screenshot the login screen so you can see the exact email they’re typing.
