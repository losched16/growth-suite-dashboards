The Family page

Article 7 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

The Family page is where parents see and update the data your school has on file about their household. This article covers what’s on the page, what parents can edit themselves, and what stays read-only.

What’s on the page

Parents

A card for each parent in the family record. Each card shows:

  • First name, last name, preferred name
  • Role (Mother, Father, Guardian, Step-parent, etc.)
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Primary parent flag (one parent per family is the primary contact)

Students

A card for each enrolled child. Each card shows:

  • First name, last name, preferred name
  • Date of birth
  • Classroom and lead teacher (if classroom is on file)
  • Enrollment status (enrolled, withdrawn, etc.)
  • Academic year

How edits flow back to your records

When a parent edits something they can update directly:

  1. The change is saved to the family record immediately.
  2. The new value is synced to that parent’s contact in Growth Suite within a few seconds.
  3. Any of your existing tags, workflows, and reporting that use that field automatically pick up the new value.

This means a parent updating their phone number once at 8pm replaces the old number everywhere — no double entry, no “remind office to update the spreadsheet.”

Email change flow

Email is special because it’s also the login. The flow goes:

  1. Parent enters their new email.
  2. A confirmation magic-link is sent to the new email.
  3. Parent clicks the link to confirm.
  4. Email is updated and the parent is logged in on the new credentials.

This prevents an accidental typo from locking a parent out.

What about adding a new parent to a family?

Today, parents can’t self-add a new parent (a new step-parent, a co-parent who wasn’t in the original enrollment, etc.). This goes through your office — parents should request it via the Messages tab and your staff updates the record from the admin side.

What about adding a new student?

Same as adding a parent — enrollment changes go through your office. Most schools handle re-enrollment, new sibling enrollment, and program changes via dedicated forms (see the Portal Forms knowledge base).

Custom fields

Some schools have additional information on file beyond the basics — allergies, custody notes, transportation method, primary language. These show on the parent side as a series of editable or read-only fields, depending on how your school configured them. Reach out to your account contact if you want to add a new parent-visible or parent-editable field.

Be thoughtful about what’s parent-editable. The default is conservative — let parents fix typos and update contact info, route everything else through the office. The reason: edits to enrollment, legal names, and classroom assignment have downstream effects (state reporting, tuition billing, classroom rosters) that benefit from a human checkpoint.
