Branding your Parent Portal

Article 4 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

The Portal is yours, not ours. Every parent who logs in should see your school’s identity — not a generic vendor screen. This article walks through every branding element you can customize and how to update them.

What you can brand

ElementWhere it appearsRecommended
Display nameHeader, footer, “Sign in to…” email subject lines.Your school’s full or short name as families know it.
LogoTop-left of the header on every page.A square PNG with transparent background, at least 128×128px.
Primary colorButtons, links, badges, accents throughout the portal.Your school’s main brand color, as a hex code (e.g. #047857).
Soft variantSubtle backgrounds (info boxes, accents).A light tint of your primary color. Defaults to a soft pastel if you don’t set one.
Foreground variantText shown on soft backgrounds.A dark variant of your primary color for readable text on the soft backgrounds.
Support emailFooter of every page; the “reach out” prompts when forms or data are missing.Your front-office email — the one parents already use.
Support phoneFooter of every page.Your school’s main phone number.
Footer textBottom of every page.Optional. Use for © copyright, address, or any persistent disclosure.

Where to set these

Branding is configured by your account team during onboarding and can be updated any time. To request a change, email your account contact with:

  • The element you want to change (logo, color, etc.)
  • The new value (a file attachment for the logo, a hex code for the color)

Updates typically go live within a business day. The change applies to every parent in your school immediately — nothing for families to do on their end.

Picking colors that work

Three colors are stored as a small palette:

  • Primary is your main brand color — it goes on buttons, links, badges. Pick a saturated, mid-darkness shade so it reads as a clear accent on a white page.
  • Soft is a very light tint of your primary — used for subtle info-box backgrounds. Roughly “your primary at 10% opacity over white.”
  • Foreground is your primary at a much darker shade — used for text inside a soft-background box. Roughly “your primary darkened by 40–50%.”

If you don’t know what the soft and foreground variants should be, just send your primary color and we’ll pick sensible variants for you.

Color examples:

  • Primary #047857 · Soft #ecfdf5 · Foreground #064e3b
  • Primary #7c3aed · Soft #f5f3ff · Foreground #4c1d95
  • Primary #dc2626 · Soft #fef2f2 · Foreground #7f1d1d

Logo guidelines

  • Aim for a transparent PNG, ideally 256×256 pixels or larger.
  • Square aspect ratio renders best in the header. If your logo is wider than tall, send both the full logo and a square “mark” version — we’ll use the mark in the header.
  • SVG works too if you have one.
  • Avoid white-on-white logos — the portal header background is white.

What parents do not see

  • The portal never shows “Growth Suite” or any other vendor name to parents.
  • The login email subject line is “Sign in to [your school name]”.
  • The sender of the magic-link email is configured per-school so it looks like it’s coming from your school’s domain.

Custom domain (optional)

By default, your portal lives on a Growth Suite subdomain. If you’d prefer a custom subdomain on your own school’s domain (e.g. family.yourschool.org), ask your account contact — it’s a one-time DNS setup and adds a few days to the timeline but makes the portal feel even more native.

Don’t over-customize. The portal is intentionally clean and consistent. Schools sometimes ask if they can change the layout, add custom menu items, or embed widgets. The answer for now is no — consistency keeps the experience reliable for families. The branding settings (logo, colors, support info) are the right lever for making it feel like your school.
