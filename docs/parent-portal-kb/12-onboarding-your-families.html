Onboarding your families to the portal

Article 12 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

A great product is wasted if families don’t know it exists. This article is the rollout playbook for getting your families into the portal — whether you’re launching to a whole school for the first time or onboarding new families as they enroll.

Before you announce

Check these five things before sending any communication to families:

  1. Branding is set. Your school’s logo, primary color, and support contact info show up correctly in the portal header and footer. (See Article 4.)
  2. All parents have valid emails on file. Audit your family records and fix anything missing — a parent without an email can’t log in.
  3. Active students are tagged to the right family. A student with no family link won’t show in the portal.
  4. Your forms are configured. If you’re launching alongside the new academic year, make sure all annual forms are live before parents log in — an empty Forms tab gives a bad first impression.
  5. You’ve walked through the portal as a real parent. Log in with a test family and click every section. See it the way they will.

The launch sequence

A simple four-step rollout that works well for most schools:

1. Send the announcement email (3–5 days before go-live)

Tell families the portal exists, why it’s coming, what they’ll be able to do, and when they can start. Don’t include the login link yet — build a little anticipation.

2. Send the “you can log in now” email (go-live day)

This is the email with the portal URL and step-by-step first-login instructions. Send it in the morning so questions land during business hours.

3. Send a 7-day follow-up

A week after go-live, send a check-in email reminding families about the portal, calling out one or two specific things they can do (“Have you signed your photo release? Takes about a minute.”), and pointing to the help contact.

4. Mention it in your normal communications

For the next month or two, add a one-line P.S. or sidebar to every parent email mentioning the portal. The goal isn’t to nag — it’s to make “the portal” a phrase families recognize.

Templates you can steal

Template 1: The announcement email (3–5 days before)

Subject: Our new Parent Portal launches next week Dear [Family Name], Starting [Monday Sept 9], [School Name] is moving to a new Parent Portal — a single place online where you can: • Sign your student in and out • Fill out school forms (permission slips, medical updates, releases) • Upload immunization records and other documents • See your tuition balance and payment plan • Message the school office directly It works on your phone, tablet, or computer — no app to download. You’ll get a follow-up email next [Monday] with your login link and a quick guide. Until then, no action is needed. Questions? Email us at [support email] or just hit reply. Thanks, [Your name] [School Name]

Template 2: The go-live email

Subject: The Parent Portal is live — here’s how to sign in Hi [Parent Name], The new [School Name] Parent Portal is live. Here’s how to get started: 1. Visit: [your portal URL] 2. Enter your email address (the one we have on file) 3. We’ll email you a one-tap sign-in link — no password to remember 4. Click the link, and you’re in Once you’re signed in, take a minute to: • Look at the Family tab — verify your contact info is correct • Visit Forms — we’ll be using these for permission slips, medical updates, and the annual paperwork • Check Settings — add your authorized pickup people (grandparents, nannies, anyone else who picks up) Save the portal URL to your phone’s home screen and you’ll be able to sign in with one tap going forward. Trouble logging in? Email [support email]. Thanks, [Your name]

Template 3: The 7-day follow-up

Subject: A week with the Parent Portal — quick check-in Hi [Parent Name], It’s been a week since the new portal launched. A quick note: If you haven’t signed in yet, today’s a great day. We’ve got a handful of annual forms loaded that we’re hoping every family completes by [date] — takes about 10 minutes for a typical family. • Sign in: [your portal URL] • Trouble? Email [support email] Some highlights of what families have been using most this week: • Check-in / check-out from the parking lot — way faster than the paper sign-in • Quick access to upload documents to the school office • Easy review of past forms and submissions Thanks — we’re here if you need anything. [Your name]

Onboarding new families (after the initial rollout)

For families enrolling after launch, the workflow is simpler:

  1. Once you’ve created their family + parent + student records in Growth Suite, send them a portal welcome email (a shortened version of the go-live template above).
  2. Reference the portal in your standard new-family welcome packet.
  3. Encourage them to log in before their student’s first day so they can complete annual forms in advance.

Common rollout pitfalls

1. Launching with empty forms

A family logs in and the Forms tab says “No forms configured.” They won’t come back. Make sure at least the annual forms are live before announcement.

2. Announcing before branding is done

A family logs in and sees a default green color instead of your school’s. Send branding to your account team a week before launch.

3. Not auditing emails

30% of families won’t be able to log in if their email on file is wrong. Run an audit in your admin dashboard first and fix the bad ones.

4. Front desk not trained

Your front desk needs to know how to answer the first wave of “how do I log in” calls. Walk them through Article 11 a day before launch.

Tracking adoption

You can see adoption in two places:

  • The admin Portal Forms Completion Grid shows how many families have submitted at least one form — a good proxy for “has this family ever logged in.”
  • The Attendance dashboard shows which families are using check-in/out — the most-used feature daily.

Aim for 80%+ of families having submitted at least one form within two weeks of launch. If you’re below that, send a personal follow-up to the holdouts — usually it’s a bad-email-on-file issue, not disinterest.
