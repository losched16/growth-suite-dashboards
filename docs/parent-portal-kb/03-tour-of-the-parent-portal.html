A tour of the Parent Portal

Article 3 of 12 · Parent Portal Knowledge Base

This article walks through every section of the portal a parent sees once they’re signed in. Use it as a reference when your front desk is on the phone with a parent who’s trying to find something.

The shell: header, nav, footer

Across every page, parents see three persistent UI elements:

  • Header with your school’s logo and name on the left, and the parent’s name + a Sign Out button on the right.
  • Navigation bar below the header with one tab per portal section: Home, Attendance, Family, Forms, Documents, Messages, Tuition, Financial Aid.
  • Footer with your school’s support email and phone (configured in branding settings).

Home

The landing page when a parent signs in. Shows a personalized summary of what needs their attention today:

  • Today’s attendance state for each student (not checked in, present, picked up)
  • Pending forms count, with quick links to fill them out
  • Recent messages from the school
  • Outstanding balance summary, if any
  • Quick action buttons for the most common tasks (check in a student, fill out a form)

Attendance

This is where parents handle daily sign-in and pickup.

  • Today’s status for each child — not checked in / present / picked up.
  • Check In — a button per child that opens the drop-off flow (sign, add note, confirm).
  • Check Out — same flow at pickup, with the added question of who is picking up (the parent, the other parent, or an authorized pickup person).
  • Attendance history — a calendar / list view of past sign-ins for each child.

See Article 5 — Attendance for the full flow.

Family

Information about the parent’s household:

  • Each parent in the family (name, role, contact info)
  • Each enrolled student (name, preferred name, date of birth, classroom)
  • Edit links so parents can update their own contact info, phone numbers, and preferred names

See Article 7 — The Family page for what parents can edit and what stays read-only.

Forms

Every form your school has configured for the portal, grouped by category, with completion status per family and per student. Parents fill them out here.

This is a whole product area on its own — see the separate Portal Forms Knowledge Base for everything about forms (per-student vs per-family, field types, signatures, etc.).

Documents

For files that don’t belong to a specific form — immunization records, custody papers, court orders, insurance cards, doctor’s notes. Parents:

  • See every file they’ve uploaded
  • Upload new files with a name, optional note, optional “for which student” and optional “for which form” tag
  • Delete files they uploaded by mistake

See Article 8 — Documents & uploads.

Messages

A direct line to the school office. Parents see:

  • The full thread of past conversation, newest first
  • A compose box for sending a new message
  • Unread badge on the navigation when there’s a new message from the school

Messages sync with the school’s admin inbox so staff can reply from there. See Article 9 — Messages.

Tuition

Shows the family’s current tuition status:

  • Current balance — what’s owed, when it’s due
  • Payment plan — if applicable, the schedule and amount per period
  • Payment history — past payments with dates and amounts
  • A pay-now link, if the school has online payment enabled

See Article 10 — Tuition & Financial Aid.

Financial Aid

The TuitionBridge module. Families who haven’t applied yet see an “Apply for tuition assistance” prompt. Families with an application in progress see their application status, required documents, and award decisions.

See Article 10 — Tuition & Financial Aid.

Settings

Account preferences and the family’s authorized pickup people list. See Article 6 — Authorized pickup people.

Mobile vs desktop

The portal is responsive — the same pages, the same data, but rearranged to fit phone screens. The nav becomes a hamburger menu, forms collapse from two columns to one, and tables become stacked card views. Most parents will use it primarily on a phone.

Tip: The fastest way to know exactly what a parent sees is to log in yourself as a test parent. Most schools keep one “test family” record in their system for this exact purpose — ask your account manager to set one up if you don’t have one.
