The parent experience

Article 9 of 12 · Portal Forms Knowledge Base

Before you build a form, it helps to know exactly what your families see. This article walks through a parent’s entire experience — from finding the form to getting a copy of their submission — click by click.

Step-by-step

1

The parent logs into their portal

They go to your branded parent portal URL and sign in. (The portal uses passwordless magic-link login — we email them a one-tap link.)

2

They click “Forms” in the side navigation

The Forms tab shows every form available, grouped by category (Registration, Medical, Permission, Release, Legal, Field Trip). Each row shows:

  • The form name and a one-line description.
  • A status pill: Complete, Pending, or X of Y students done for per-student forms.
  • A green badge if there’s a fee attached.

A progress bar across the top shows what percentage of forms are complete for the year.

3

They click into a specific form

The form opens with the title, your description, and any draft warnings at the top. For a per-student form, they see a horizontal student picker first — they tap the child this submission is for.

The form fields appear, with the parent’s name, the student’s name, today’s date, and any health profile data already pre-filled. They only have to fill in what’s actually new.

4

They sign and submit

For a drawn signature, they sign in the box with their finger or mouse, then tap Lock signature — the box turns green. For a typed signature, they type their full legal name in the field above the date.

They tap Submit form. Within a second, they’re redirected to their submission history with a green “Submitted!” banner at the top.

5

They can review or print their submission

The history page lists every submission in their family’s record. Each entry has a View / print button that opens the printable view. From there, the parent can save it as a PDF using their browser’s built-in print dialog.

6

If the form is one-per-year and already submitted, they see a “Complete” state

Forms that only allow one submission per year (the default for most forms) show a green confirmation panel instead of an empty form: “You’ve completed this form for the 2025-26 year.” with a link to view their submission.

What works on mobile

The portal is designed mobile-first. Every form type works on a phone:

  • Drawn signatures use the touchscreen — parents sign with their finger like a credit card receipt.
  • File uploads open the phone’s camera or photo library, so parents can snap a photo of an immunization card without leaving the form.
  • Date pickers use the phone’s native calendar picker.

Reminders & nudges

The portal doesn’t spam parents about pending forms by default. Reminder emails are configured separately through your Growth Suite workflows (see Article 8 — Growth Suite writeback). A common pattern:

  • When a form is added to the system, send an announcement email.
  • Two weeks before the academic year starts, send a reminder to families with incomplete forms.
  • Set up a workflow that fires only when a specific form’s completion date field is empty.

Tip: Always log in as a real parent (using a test account if you have one) to preview your forms before activating them. The parent view is different from the admin preview — you want to see the experience your families will actually have.
