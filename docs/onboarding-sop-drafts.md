# Onboarding SOP drafts — for Freshdesk

*First-draft help articles for each school-facing onboarding step. Paste each
into Freshdesk, refine the wording/screenshots, then map the article URL to its
task at `/admin/onboarding/guides`.*

**Accuracy note:**
- **Growth Suite** steps (branding, portal, dashboards, forms, intake, roster,
  logo) describe screens in *this* product and are accurate to how it's built —
  add screenshots and you're done.
- **GHL** steps (calendar, social, reviews, follow-up) describe GoHighLevel,
  whose UI changes often. Treat the click-paths as a skeleton and verify the
  exact menu/button labels against your current GHL before publishing.

Each article follows the same shape: **what it does → steps → you're done when.**
Keep that consistency — it makes the checklist feel like one course.

## YOUR DATA (intake steps — done on your onboarding page)

### Your grade levels
**What this does:** The grade levels you enter here become the options on every
student record, dashboard filter, and form across your account — so it's worth
getting the names right once.

**Steps:**
1. On your onboarding page, find **"Your grade levels."**
2. Type one grade level per line, exactly as you want it to appear
   (e.g. `Toddler`, `Primary`, `Lower Elementary`, `Upper Elementary`).
3. Use the names your school actually uses — parents and staff will see them.
4. Click **Submit**.

**Tips:** Keep them consistent (don't mix "Pre-K" and "PreK"). You can update
the list later — we'll re-sync it.

**You're done when:** the step shows **Submitted**, and once our team applies it
you'll see **Applied to your account ✓**.

### Your programs
**What this does:** The programs families can enroll in (e.g. Half Day, Full
Day, Extended Day). Used on student records, dashboards, and enrollment forms.

**Steps:** Same as grade levels — one program per line, then **Submit**.

**You're done when:** the step shows **Submitted / Applied ✓**.

### Your daily schedules
**What this does:** The schedule options you offer (e.g. `2 Days`, `3 Days`,
`5 Days`, or `Half Day / Full Day`). Drives scheduling fields and forms.

**Steps:** One schedule per line, then **Submit**.

### Your classrooms / homerooms
**What this does:** Your classroom or homeroom names — used for per-classroom
dashboards and rosters.

**Steps:** One classroom per line (e.g. `Room A`, `Sunflower Room`), then
**Submit**.

### Submit your student roster
**What this does:** Instead of typing families in one by one, you send us your
current roster once and we import everyone.

**Before you start:** Export a current student/family list from your existing
system (or fill in our template). A CSV or Excel file works.

**Helpful columns (don't worry if you're missing some):** family/last name,
primary parent name + email, student first + last name, date of birth,
grade level, program, classroom.

**Steps:**
1. On your onboarding page, find **"Submit your student roster."**
2. Click **Choose file** and select your CSV/Excel export.
3. Click **Upload.**
4. That's it — our team reviews and imports it. You'll see the **Roster
   imported** step turn green when your families are loaded.

**Tips:** One row per student is easiest. If you have two parents per family,
include both emails if you can.

**You're done when:** your file shows **uploaded**, and the **Roster imported**
step (handled by us) flips to done.

### Submit your logo
**What this does:** Your logo appears at the top of your parent portal.

**Steps:**
1. Find **"Submit your logo."**
2. Upload a **PNG or SVG** (a transparent-background PNG looks best).
3. Click **Upload.**

**You're done when:** the file shows uploaded. It'll appear in your portal once
branding is set.

## CONFIGURATION (done in your Growth Suite dashboard)

### Branding configured
**What this does:** Sets your portal's logo and colors so it looks like *your*
school, not generic software.

**Steps:**
1. Click **"Do it in your dashboard →"** on this step (or open **Settings**).
2. Under **Branding**, set your **Display name**, paste your **Logo URL**, and
   pick your **primary color** (and the soft/foreground shades if you want).
3. Add a **support email** and **phone** so parents know who to contact.
4. Click **Save.**

**You're done when:** your logo + colors show in the parent portal. This step
turns green automatically once a logo is set.

### Parent portal set up
**What this does:** Sets your academic year and controls which sections parents
see in their portal.

**Steps:**
1. Open **Settings** (the "Do it in your dashboard →" link).
2. Set your **academic year** (e.g. `2026-27`).
3. Under **Portal menus**, turn on the sections you want parents to see and turn
   off the ones you don't. (Home is always shown.)
4. Optionally set the **portal gate** — which admissions stage a family must
   reach before they can create a portal login.
5. Click **Save.**

**You're done when:** your academic year is set — the step turns green
automatically.

### Dashboards set up
**What this does:** Adds the staff dashboards (Family Hub, Student Roster,
Enrollment Hub, per-classroom views, etc.) your team uses day to day.

**Before you start:** Your roster should be imported first, so the dashboards
have data.

**Steps:**
1. Click **"Do it in your dashboard →"** (opens **Add a dashboard**).
2. Pick dashboards from the **template gallery** — start with Family Hub and
   Student Roster.
3. Use the **classroom hubs generator** to create one dashboard per classroom
   in a click.
4. Open any dashboard and use **Edit layout** to arrange or add widgets.

**You're done when:** at least one dashboard exists — the step turns green
automatically.

### Forms published
**What this does:** The forms parents fill out (emergency medical, enrollment
agreement, pickup authorization, etc.), built from templates.

**Steps:**
1. Click **"Do it in your dashboard →"** (opens **New form**).
2. Start from a **template** (pickup authorization, medication consent, photo
   release, field trip, emergency/medical, or blank).
3. Use the **drag-and-drop builder** to adjust fields, add conditional logic,
   and map fields to your contact record.
4. Set **who sees this form**, preview it, then **Publish.**

**You're done when:** at least one form is **published** (drafts don't count).

## MARKETING & GROWTH (done in GoHighLevel)

> ⚠️ Verify the exact GHL menu/button names against your current account before
> publishing these — GHL's UI changes. The flow below is the skeleton.

### Set up your tour booking calendar
**What this does:** Lets prospective families book a tour or intro call
themselves, so inquiries convert without phone tag.

**Steps:**
1. In your dashboard, go to **Calendars** (under Settings / Calendars).
2. Create a new calendar — name it something parents recognize
   (e.g. "Book a Tour at [School]").
3. Set your **availability** (days/times you offer tours), tour **length**, and
   any **buffer** between tours.
4. Connect the staff member's **Google/Outlook calendar** so booked tours land
   on their real calendar and double-bookings are blocked.
5. Copy the calendar's **booking link** (or embed it on your website / add it to
   your funnel).
6. Optionally add a **confirmation + reminder** message so families show up.

**Tips:** One simple "Book a Tour" calendar beats five confusing ones. Add the
link to your website header and your Instagram bio.

**You're done when:** you can open your booking link and schedule a test tour,
and it appears on the connected staff calendar. Then mark this step done.

### Connect your social accounts
**What this does:** Links your Facebook, Instagram, and Google Business profiles
so you can post from one place and capture leads that come through them.

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Settings → Integrations** (or **Social Planner → connect accounts**).
2. Connect **Facebook** — authorize the Page (and the linked **Instagram**
   business account).
3. Connect **Google Business Profile** so reviews and messages flow in.
4. Confirm each shows **Connected**.
5. Optionally schedule a first test post from the **Social Planner** to confirm
   posting works.

**Tips:** You'll need to be an admin of the Facebook Page and have Instagram set
to a Business/Creator account for the connection to work.

**You're done when:** Facebook, Instagram, and Google show **Connected**. Mark
this step done.

### Turn on review requests
**What this does:** Automatically asks happy families for a Google review, which
lifts your reputation and helps new families find you.

**Steps:**
1. Make sure **Google Business Profile** is connected (previous step).
2. Go to **Reputation → Settings** (or the Reviews section).
3. Turn on **review requests** and choose how they send (email, SMS, or both).
4. Personalize the **request message** in your school's voice.
5. Decide the **trigger** — e.g. after a family enrolls, or send manually to a
   short list to start.
6. Send yourself a **test** request and confirm the review link opens your
   Google review page.

**Tips:** Start by manually requesting from your happiest current families to
build a base of reviews, then let it run automatically.

**You're done when:** a test review request sends and links to your Google
review page. Mark this step done.

### Turn on lead follow-up
**What this does:** Automatically nurtures new inquiries (email/SMS) toward
booking a tour, so no lead goes cold while you're busy running the school.

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Automation → Workflows.**
2. Start from a **new-lead / nurture** template (or the one we've set up in your
   snapshot).
3. Set the **trigger** — e.g. a new contact from your tour form or website.
4. Review the **email/SMS steps** and edit them to sound like your school.
5. Make sure the sequence points families to your **tour booking calendar.**
6. **Publish** the workflow and send a **test** lead through it.

**Tips:** Keep the first message fast and personal ("Thanks for your interest in
[School]! Want to book a tour?"). Short sequences that book tours beat long ones.

**You're done when:** a test lead triggers the follow-up and you receive the
messages. Mark this step done.

## LAUNCH

### Confirm everything looks right
**What this does:** Your final review before your portal goes live to parents.

**Steps:**
1. Click through your **dashboards** — do your families and students look right?
2. Open your **forms** — do they have the fields you need?
3. Check your **branding** in the parent portal.
4. When you're happy, **check this step done.**

**You're done when:** you've reviewed your setup and checked the box. Our team
does a final pass next.
